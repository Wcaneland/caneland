Governance

The Constitution

Caneland operates under a written constitution with clear separation of powers.

Article 1 – The People

All power in the state derives from the people.

Article 2 – The President

Elected directly by majority vote for a 1-year term.

Article 3 – The Court

A Chief Justice and several judges.

Article 4 – Checks and Balances

The Chief Justice may remove the President from office if the President violates the rules of the country. Other judges may raise an objection if it is well-founded.

Article 5 – Snap Elections

60%+ popular demand forces the Court to approve a snap election.

Article 6 – Test Elections

A non-binding test election is held on the 26th of every month.

Article 7 – National Holiday

The 26th of January is the founding day of Caneland and a national holiday. A day of remembrance is also held on the 26th of every other month.

Protection of the Constitution

The Constitution may only be amended if 70% of the people and a majority of the judges on the Court agree. Simple amendments by the President or individual persons are not permitted.
