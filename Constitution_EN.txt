================================================================
                   CONSTITUTION OF CANELAND
================================================================

Article 1 – The People
All power in the state emanates from the people. The people elect the President and vote on important decisions, including the confirmation of the Chief Justice and potential new elections.

Article 2 – The President
The President is elected directly by the people. Whoever receives more than 50% of the votes wins. The term of office is 1 year. There is no limit to the number of possible terms of office.
Anyone can run for election. The court reviews the candidacy and decides whether it is approved.
The President forms the government and decides which offices exist, who fills them, and what their duties are. This is usually announced before the election so that the people know which government concept they are voting for.

Article 3 – The Court
There is a court consisting of a Chief Justice and several other judges. Judges can apply to the court through the normal application process.
Within 3 days after their election, the President proposes a judge as Chief Justice. As a rule, this proposal is already announced before the election results. The people then vote on this proposal.
If the President does not make a proposal within 3 days, the people decide independently. During the transition period, the previous Chief Justice remains in office until a successor is confirmed.
The election of the Chief Justice is supervised by the other judges.

[Article on New Elections / Snap Elections]
• If [more than 60%] of the people vote for a new election, the court must approve it in almost all cases. A rejection is only possible in very rare exceptional cases, for example if there are no approved opposing candidates available.
• If between 20% and 60% vote for a new election, the court may approve it, but is not obliged to do so.
• If fewer than 20% vote for a new election, it will not be approved.

Article 6 – Test Elections
On the 26th of each month, a test election takes place. This has no effect on the government and serves solely for practice and civic engagement. An exception is January 26, on which the official elections take place.

Article 6B – Test Elections (Addition)
(1) Test elections serve exclusively to determine public satisfaction and have no direct legal consequences.
(2) All persons living in or recognized in Caneland may participate in test elections.
(3) The results are published separately:
    a) Votes of the citizens of Caneland.
    b) Votes of all participating persons.

Article 7 – National Holiday
January 26 is the founding day of Caneland and a national holiday. To commemorate this day, a day of remembrance additionally takes place on the 26th of every other month.

Protection of the Constitution
The constitution may only be amended if 70% of the people and a majority of the judges in the court agree.
