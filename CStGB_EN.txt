================================================================
              CODE OF LAWS OF CANELAND
             Democracy and Rule of Law
================================================================

================================================================
BOOK I — CONSTITUTION / BASIC ORDER
================================================================

Art. 1 (Form of State)
Caneland is a democracy and a state governed by the rule of law.

Art. 2 (State Authority)
State authority emanates from the people and is exercised through legislation, administration, and judiciary (separation of powers).

Art. 3 (Basic Rights)
(1) Every person in Caneland has the right to dignity, physical integrity, and the free development of their personality.
(2) No one may be discriminated against on the basis of origin, gender, language, homeland, belief, sexuality, or religious/political views.
(3) Freedom of expression, assembly, and religion are guaranteed, provided they do not violate the rights of others.
(4) Property is protected; expropriation only with compensation and on a legal basis.

Art. 4 (Citizenship)
(1) A person becomes a citizen of Caneland through birth, grant, or naturalization.
(2) All citizens are equal before the law.

BOOK II — CRIMINAL LAW (Canelandic Criminal Code, CStGB)
================================================================

§1 Principle
An act shall only be punished if it was defined by law before it was committed (nulla poena sine lege).

§2 Criminal Responsibility
Anyone who can think and express themselves independently is criminally responsible. There is no statutory age. (To be decided by the court for each specific case)

§3 Self-Defense and Emergency Aid
(1) Anyone who wards off an ongoing unlawful attack on themselves or another person does not act unlawfully.
(2) The defense must be necessary and must not be manifestly disproportionate.

--- SECTION A: OFFENSES AGAINST THE PERSON ---

§10 Bodily Harm
(1) Anyone who physically ill-treats or harms the health of another person shall be punished with a fine or community service.
(2) Severe bodily harm (loss of a body part, permanent disfigurement, danger to life) shall be punished more severely, potentially with deprivation of liberty.

§11 Threats
Anyone who threatens another person with the commission of a crime in order to frighten them commits an offense.

§12 Coercion
Anyone who unlawfully forces another person to act, suffer, or refrain from an action through force or threats shall be punished.

§13 Stalking
Anyone who persistently and unauthorizedly stalks a person (e.g., repeated contact attempts despite refusal, following, spying) shall be punished with a fine, contact prohibition, or deprivation of liberty.

§14 Harassment
(1) Anyone who violates the dignity of another person through repeated, unwanted behavior (verbal, written, digital, or physical) or creates an intimidating, hostile, or humiliating environment commits an administrative offense / crime.
(2) Sexual Harassment: Anyone who harasses a person through unwanted sexually oriented actions, remarks, touching, or showing pornographic content shall be punished with a fine, ban from public/commercial premises, or deprivation of liberty.
(3) Online Harassment / Cyberbullying: Paragraphs 1–2 apply accordingly to digital communication channels.
(4) A warning or a clearly expressed "No" by the affected person is sufficient for further behavior to be deemed "unwanted".

§15 Discrimination
Anyone who discriminates against a person in access to business, housing, education, or public services on the basis of a protected characteristic (Art. 3 Para. 2) shall be fined; in the case of repetition, a business ban shall be imposed.

§16 Sexual Offenses
(1) Sexual acts without the consent of the affected person are punishable and shall be sanctioned with deprivation of liberty.
(2) Sexual acts with or in front of minors are strictly prohibited without exception and shall be punished with the maximum sentence.
(3) Consent can be revoked at any time; consent once given does not apply continuously.

§17 Insult / Defamation / Slander
(1) Insult: Derogatory statement made to/about a person.
(2) Defamation: Assertion of untrue, defamatory facts.
(3) Slander: Knowingly false, defamatory assertion.
Penalties: Fine, public retraction, in severe cases deprivation of liberty.

§18 Murder
(1) Anyone who intentionally kills a human being shall be punished with deprivation of liberty.
(2) In particularly severe cases, the maximum penalty of general criminal law may be imposed.

--- SECTION B: OFFENSES AGAINST PROPERTY ---

§20 Theft
Anyone who takes a movable item belonging to another person with the intention of unlawfully appropriating it shall be punished.

§21 Embezzlement
Anyone who unlawfully appropriates an item belonging to another person that has been entrusted to them.

§22 Damage to Property
Anyone who damages or destroys property belonging to another (incl. public facilities, infrastructure) is liable for damages and shall additionally be fined.

§23 Fraud
Anyone who obtains a financial advantage to the detriment of another by deception shall be punished. Includes identity theft and fraudulent transactions in digital space.

§24 Extortion
Anyone who forces a person into an action harmful to property through violence or threat.

§25 Trespassing
Unauthorized entry or remaining on third-party premises/property despite being asked to leave.

--- SECTION C: OFFENSES AGAINST THE PUBLIC ---

§30 Forgery of Documents
Forgery of official documents, IDs, or contracts.

§31 Usurping Public Authority
Anyone who unauthorizedly presents themselves as an official of Caneland.

§32 Disturbance of the Peace
Unreasonable noise during nighttime (10 PM – 6 AM).

§33 Environmental Protection
Anyone who illegally disposes of waste, pollutes water, or damages nature protection areas shall be fined and required to restore the area.

§34 Bribery and Corruption
(1) Any public official who demands, accepts the promise of, or receives an unjustified advantage for an official act shall be punished.
(2) Likewise, anyone who offers, promises, or grants such an advantage to an official to influence a decision shall be punished.

--- SECTION D: PENALTIES AND EXECUTION ---

§40 Types of Penalties
1. Warning
2. Community service
3. Business/activity ban
4. Premise ban
5. Deprivation of liberty (only for serious crimes, time-limited)
6. Revocation of citizenship privileges (in extreme cases)

§41 Sentencing
Factors considered: Severity of the act, intent/negligence, prior convictions, restitution, remorse, age of the perpetrator.

================================================================
BOOK III — COMMERCIAL LAW
================================================================

§50 Freedom of Trade
Every citizen may carry out a trade unless legal obstacles (e.g., relevant prior convictions, lack of minimum requirements) prevent it.

§50a Trade Promotion
(1) Caneland promotes new businesses through state advertising via the AMC (Office for Media in Caneland).
(2) Newly registered businesses are officially advertised by the state, e.g., via state media channels.
(3) The promotion applies to all registered businesses and ends after a period set by the TUG.

§51 Trade Registration Requirement
(1) Every trade must be registered with the relevant trade office before starting activity (name, activity, location).
(2) Registration is free or for a small fee.
(3) Breach of registration duty: fine, repeated breach results in business prohibition.

§52 Bookkeeping Requirement
Traders must document income/expenses comprehensibly, retained for at least 2 years.

§53 State Contribution
(1) All citizens of Caneland make a state contribution in the form of mandatory social work, unless they can prove they have regular employment.
(2) Anyone actively working (agriculture, processing, trade, or authority work) is exempt from the state contribution.
(3) Type and extent of social work is determined by the state.
(4) No trade tax exists.

§54 Consumer Protection
(1) Traders must display prices transparently.
(2) A right of withdrawal applies to distance sales.
(3) Defect rights: exchange, repair, or refund for defective goods/services.
(4) All items for sale must be approved by the state consumer protection authority (TUG) and marked with a valid badge.
(See TUG Guidelines)

§55 Trade Prohibition
The trade office can prohibit a trade in case of: repeated fraud, discrimination (§15), tax evasion, safety violations.

§56 Occupational Safety (when employing others)
Traders with employees must ensure minimum wage, reasonable working hours, and protection against harassment (§14) in the workplace.

§57 Special Trades
Certain activities (gastronomy, financial services, trade in sensitive goods) require an additional permit/license from the trade office.

================================================================
BOOK IV — ADMINISTRATIVE AND AUTHORIZATION LAW
================================================================

§60 Permits and Authorizations
(1) Certain activities (e.g., special trades, events, construction projects) require a state permit.
(2) Permits can be temporarily revoked in case of abuse or court order; revocation must be justified and can be appealed.

§62 Right of Assembly and Events
(1) Public assemblies require registration (at least 90 minutes beforehand with the public order office).
(2) Assemblies must not call for violence or contain hate speech (§15, §17).

§63 Building and Property Law
Construction projects require a building permit; minimum distances and safety requirements must be observed.
(See TUG Guidelines)

§64 Data Protection
(1) Personal data may only be collected and stored for specific purposes.
(2) Every person has the right to information, correction, and deletion of their data.
(3) Data leaks must be reported immediately to affected persons and the state.

§65 Animal Welfare
Anyone who tortures or neglects an animal shall be punished with a fine and revocation of animal ownership permission.

================================================================
BOOK V — PROCEDURAL LAW
================================================================

§70 Reporting
Crimes and administrative offenses can be reported to the competent authority, informally or in writing.

§71 Procedural Principles
(1) Presumption of innocence until proven guilty.
(2) Right to a hearing and defense.
(3) Right to complaint/appeal against decisions.

§72 Mediation
In case of harassment (§14), neighbor disputes, or minor property damage, conciliation should be offered before formal proceedings.

§73 Catalog of Fines
See separate Canelandic Catalog of Fines (CKat).
