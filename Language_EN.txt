---Grammar & Rules---

Sentence Structure-Rulenlong

Declarative sentence: [Sentence] + [Time-word]
Interrogative sentence: Activity, who/what (how) (where)?
Negation: "Nie" + who/what + Activity (how) (where)

Article Rules-Rulen
lö = 1 (definite/indefinite article, singular)
de = 2 (article for plural / multiple things)
Articles always stand before nouns and people.
For multiple things, the form of no word changes – only the number is placed in front of the word (or the word for "multiple": de).

Tense Rules-Rulen
Tense is expressed by a time-word at the beginning of the sentence:
baff = in the past
nau = now/currently
dann = later/afterwards

Punctuation Rules-Rulen
. = always (only) at the end of the sentence
, = only for lists
? = replaces the period at the end of a question mark (so it only stands at the end, not at the beginning)

Spelling Rules-Rulen
Everything is written in lowercase, except the first letter at the beginning of the sentence.

Personal Pronoun Rules-Rulen
ick = I
tu = you
lö = he / he (Article Singular)
de = she (singular) / de (Article)
woek = we
ir = you (plural)
dey = they
(Forms never change – always the same)
